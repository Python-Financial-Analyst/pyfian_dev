yield_to_maturity(price: float, settlement_date: str | pandas.Timestamp | None = None, adjust_to_business_days: bool | None = None, day_count_convention: str | pyfian.utils.day_count.DayCountBase | None = None, following_coupons_day_count: str | pyfian.utils.day_count.DayCountBase | None = None, yield_calculation_convention: str | None = None) → float[source]ÔÉÅ

Estimate the yield to maturity (YTM) using the xirr function from pyfian.time_value.irr.

The YTM is the internal rate of return (IRR) of the instrument‚Äôs cash flows, assuming the instrument is held to maturity.

It is the discount rate that makes the present value of the instrument‚Äôs cash flows equal to its price for a given set of cash flows and settlement date.

The YTM is calculated by solving the equation:

\[P = \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{C_t}{(1 + YTM)^{(t+1)}}\]

where:

  • \(P\) is the price of the instrument

  • \(C_t\) is the cash flow at time \(t\), where \(t\) is the time in years from the settlement date

  • \(YTM\) is the yield to maturity

  • \(T\) is the total number of periods

The times to payments are calculated from the settlement date to each payment date and need not be integer values.

Parameters:

  • price (float) ‚Äì Price of the instrument.

  • settlement_date (str or datetime-like, optional) ‚Äì Settlement date. Defaults to issue date.

  • adjust_to_business_days (bool, optional) ‚Äì Whether to adjust payment dates to business days. Defaults to value of self.adjust_to_business_days.

  • day_count_convention (str or DayCountBase, optional) ‚Äì Day count convention. Defaults to value of self.day_count_convention.

  • following_coupons_day_count (str or DayCountBase, optional) ‚Äì Day count convention for following coupons. Defaults to value of self.following_coupons_day_count.

  • yield_calculation_convention (str, optional) ‚Äì Yield calculation convention. Defaults to value of self.yield_calculation_convention.

Returns:

ytm ‚Äì Estimated yield to maturity as a decimal.

Return type:

float

Raises:

ValueError ‚Äì If price is not set or YTM calculation does not converge.

Examples

>>> bill = MoneyMarketInstrument('2020-01-01', '2025-07-01', 5, 1)
>>> bill.yield_to_maturity(price=95)
np.float64(0.06100197251858131)
